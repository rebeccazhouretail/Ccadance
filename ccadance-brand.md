# CCadance in Retail — Brand Foundation
*Written: 13 April 2026*

---

## The Clarity

**JYS Retail** = the company, the school, the operator. B2B, brand-facing, Rebecca-facing.

**CCadance** = the product, the ecosystem, the name that touches the SA directly. B2C, SA-facing, public.

The business logic:
- Rebecca builds her voice on social media → SAs discover CCadance → they learn / get hired → brands pay JYS for the talent → revenue
- Not selling to companies. Selling to people. People who want to be better at their profession.
- The KOL model applied to retail education: traffic becomes trust, trust becomes revenue.

## Mission

To make the Sales Associate profession one that people choose with pride — by giving every SA the tools, the language, and the community to prove their own value.

中文：让销售顾问成为一个值得骄傲的选择——为每一位销售顾问提供工具、语言与社群，让他们能够证明自己的价值。

## Vision

A world where the best Sales Associate in the room is known by name — not by the brand on the door.

中文：让最优秀的销售顾问因自身而被记住——而不是因为身后那块品牌的牌子。

## Values

**1. The SA is the expert.**
Not a helper. Not a shop assistant. An expert who carries client relationships, product knowledge, and commercial judgment — and deserves to be recognised as such.

**2. Own your clients. Own your future.**
The SA who builds a portable client portfolio is never dependent on one brand, one manager, or one store. CCadance teaches this from day one.

**3. Small actions, compounded.**
One contact. One story. One follow-up. These are not small things. They are the unit of everything that grows. We build habits, not shortcuts.

**4. Knowledge is the product.**
CCadance does not sell promises. It sells structured knowledge — tools, frameworks, and systems that a real SA can use on the floor tomorrow.

**5. The community is the proof.**
When SAs who used CCadance get hired, perform better, and build their own client portfolios — that is the only marketing that matters.

## Community Strategy
*Added: 14 April 2026 — inspired by Professor Xiang Biao, Zhejiang University lecture on anxiety and community*

**The problem it solves**

A Sales Associate works alone in the room with the client. Their anxiety is individual — am I good enough, will I hit my target, what happens if the store closes. But the problem is not individual. It is structural. The profession has no community that holds its standards, shares its knowledge, or recognises its best people. CCadance in Retail builds that community.

**The principle**

Not one big family. Small groups. A group of 200 is noise. A group of 5–7 is a community. People find belonging in small, specific, real connections — not in large platforms where everyone is a stranger.

**The structure**

```
CCadance in Retail  (the big group — shared identity, shared values)
        │
        ├── Focus Group  (5–7 SAs, same city or same brand tier)
        ├── Focus Group  (5–7 SAs, same product category expertise)
        ├── Focus Group  (5–7 SAs, same career stage — e.g. 2–5 years)
        └── Focus Group  (5–7 SAs, same language — CN / EN / multilingual)
```

Each small group has:
- A shared identity — what they have in common
- A shared rhythm — regular check-in, weekly or biweekly
- A shared challenge — one thing they are all working on together

The big group gives them the brand. The small group gives them the belonging.

**The one principle**

Belonging comes before performance. An SA who feels they belong to a community that sees them will perform better — not because they are told to, but because they do not want to let the group down. This is what "shark mentality" misses entirely. The shark is alone. The community stays.

**References**
- Professor Xiang Biao — concept of *fujin* (附近 / the nearby): rebuild connection with the people immediately around you. This is the philosophical root of the CCadance community model.
- LVMH retail community model — small brand communities with shared rituals (morning briefing, training, seasonal events). The ritual creates the rhythm. The rhythm creates the belonging.
- WeChat group as community unit — in China, real communities live in groups of 20–50. Not 500. The intimacy is the product.
